# Street Legacy — Product Overview

## Product Identity
**Name**: Street Legacy  
**Type**: Browser-based life simulation game  
**Platform**: Web browser (desktop and mobile — no install required)  
**Comparable**: BitLife — but deeper, more system-rich, with cinematic consequence UI  

## Positioning Statement
Street Legacy is the most interconnected life simulator on the web. Players are born into a neighborhood — Hood to Luxury District — and navigate decades of choices about school, work, crime, love, money, and family. Unlike lighter life sims, every system talks to every other: school records feed career ceilings, criminal history shapes legal outcomes, neighborhood defines starting opportunity, and a family's wealth and reputation carry into the next generation. One death is just the beginning of the next life.

## Target Audience
- Casual-to-mid-core browser and mobile gamers, primarily ages 16–35
- Fans of life simulation games: BitLife, The Sims, Papers Please
- Players drawn to consequence-driven storytelling and emergent narrative
- Fans of urban street-life and "street-to-riches" arcs
- Replayability seekers who experiment with radically different life paths

## Brand & Tone
**Visual identity**: Gritty, editorial, and urban-aspirational. Dark charcoal and slate palette; amber gold as the primary accent. Cinematic street photography mood — raw but aspirational.  
**Voice**: Direct and consequence-first. Events are stated plainly, without sugarcoating or moralizing. The game respects player intelligence.  
**Core feeling**: "What would happen if I'd made different choices?" — the authentic pull of a well-designed life sim.

## Core Game Loop
1. **Create** — Build a character: full appearance, starting neighborhood, family wealth, talents, personality traits
2. **Live** — Age up one year at a time; each year surfaces randomized life events requiring player decisions
3. **Compound** — Stats and reputation shift based on choices; systems cascade into each other
4. **Die or Continue** — Review the Legacy Score on the End of Life screen; continue as a child to carry the legacy forward

## Save System
5 save slots + autosave every 5 minutes + manual save + backup system

## Key Systems

### Neighborhood & Dynamic City
Five neighborhood types — Hood, Suburbs, Downtown, Rural, Luxury District — each with distinct crime rates, housing costs, school quality, and job availability. All city metrics shift year-by-year: economy, crime rates, school ratings, housing prices, job market, population.

### Education
- 6 school district tiers: Failing → Struggling → Average → Good → Excellent → Elite
- 8 school types: Public · Charter · Private · Religious · Military Academy · Boarding · Alternative · Reform
- Interactive class actions: Attend · Participate · Ask Questions · Study · Joke Around · Use Phone · Sleep · Leave Early · Skip Class · Skip School
- Teacher and student interactions; school consequences: Warning → Detention → Suspension → Expulsion → Transfer
- School reputations: Honor Student · Popular · Athlete · Class Clown · Rebel · Troublemaker · Dropout Risk

### Relationships
Family (parents, siblings, grandparents, cousins) · Social (friends, best friends, enemies, rivals) · Romance (dating → engagement → marriage → divorce) · Children (pregnancy, adoption, raising children, grandchildren)

### Careers & Business
- 100+ careers across all income brackets: Retail Worker, Delivery Driver, Teacher, Nurse, Police Officer, Firefighter, Mechanic, Engineer, Lawyer, Doctor, Pilot, Scientist, Athlete, Musician, Actor, Politician, CEO, Business Owner, and more
- Business system: start a business, hire/fire employees, expand locations, marketing, taxes, bankruptcy

### Property & Vehicles
- Properties: Apartment · House · Mansion · Farm · Penthouse — with full bill system (rent, mortgage, water/electric/internet/phone bills, insurance)
- Vehicles: 30+ garage slots; Economy → Sports → Luxury → Muscle → Trucks → Motorcycles; fuel, maintenance, insurance, registration, vehicle value tracking
- Driving events: traffic stops, accidents, tickets, breakdowns, road trips

### Items
Jewelry (chains, watches, rings, bracelets) · Clothing (shoes, jackets, hats, designer clothing) · Electronics (phones, tablets, computers, consoles)

### Legal & Court System *(Signature Feature)*
The arrest-to-courtroom pipeline is a fully animated, multi-screen UI sequence with all buttons pressable:

1. **Police investigation** → evidence collection → witness statements → warrant → arrest notification popup
2. **Bail screen**: Pay Bail / Stay In Custody / Request Bail Reduction
3. **Lawyer selection screen**: 5 lawyers ranked low-to-high by price and reputation
   - Public Defender (free · lowest rep) → Budget Lawyer → Standard Lawyer → Experienced Lawyer → Elite Lawyer ($$$$· highest rep)
4. **Courtroom UI**: Displays charges, judge name, selected lawyer, criminal history; player chooses plea (Guilty / Not Guilty / No Contest)
5. **Trial resolution**: Weighted by evidence strength, witnesses, lawyer skill, judge strictness, criminal history
6. **Verdict and sentence**: Dismissed · Not Guilty · Guilty → Fine / Community Service / Probation / House Arrest / Detention / Jail / Prison

**Court types**: Juvenile · Municipal · Criminal · Civil · Appeals  
**Post-conviction systems**:
- Probation: community service, check-ins, school and employment requirements
- House arrest: electronic monitoring, curfew, check-ins, court reviews, violation consequences
- Parole: hearings, early release opportunities, behavior tracking
- Appeals: file appeal → new trial / reduced sentence / conviction overturned

**Prison**: Minimum → Medium → Maximum → Supermax; prison jobs, education programs, recreation, prison reputation  
**Criminal records**: Separate juvenile and adult records; minor juvenile offenses may be sealed at age 18

### Contraband & Possession
Categories: Marijuana · Illegal Pills · Counterfeit Goods · Stolen Property  
Storage options: On person · At home · In vehicle (each with distinct risk profiles)  
Consequences: Citations → Fines → Probation → Arrest → Court Cases

### Health
Doctors · Hospitals · Injuries · Illnesses · Mental Health · Fitness and Exercise

### Reputation System
Possible reputations: Model Citizen · Popular · Athlete · Business Leader · Celebrity · Troublemaker · Repeat Offender

### Achievements & Statistics
- 500+ achievements spanning every life path
- Statistics page: Net Worth · Career Earnings · Education · Criminal History · Businesses Owned · Properties Owned · Vehicles Owned · Relationships

### Generational System
On character death: End of Life screen shows Age, Cause of Death, Net Worth, Career Summary, Criminal History, Family Tree, Legacy Score, Achievements Earned.  
Continue as a child — family wealth, reputation, and legacy score transfer to the next generation. Multi-generation family dynasties are the long-game goal.

## Strategic Principles
1. **Consequence cascades**: No decision is isolated. School trouble limits careers; criminal records block housing and employment; neighborhood shapes starting opportunity.
2. **UI clarity**: Every button is fully pressable with immediate visual feedback. Signature moments (arrest → bail → lawyer selection → courtroom) get dedicated immersive multi-screen UI flows, not one-liner text prompts.
3. **Replayability by design**: Randomized events + 5 neighborhoods + 5 wealth tiers + 6 talents + 9 personality traits + 6 start ages = virtually infinite unique life stories.
4. **Street-to-riches authenticity**: The game honors the difficulty of rising from poverty and the ease of falling from wealth — without moralizing.
5. **Generational depth**: One life's end is the next life's beginning. Stakes, reputation, and wealth carry forward, creating true dynasty gameplay.
